A Slice of Genius  In Christian art, St. Jerome, the early medieval theologian who translated the Bible into Latin, is often depicted at his desk accompanied by a resting lion, whose loyalty he won by removing a thorn from its paw. Venetian Renaissance artist Vittore Carpaccio (ca. 1460-ca. 1526), a genius of uncommon gentleness, playfully re-imagined this iconographic tradition as winsome drollery in “St. Jerome Leading the Lion Into the Monastery” (1502-08), a painting from a series on the life of St. Jerome for the Scuola di S.
