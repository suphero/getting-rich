Children’s Books: Of Moonstones and Other Mysteries  The year is 1826, when two unusual young ladies become fast friends and solve a crime in “The Case of the Missing Moonstone” (Random House, 240 pages, $16.99), the first volume in a planned “Wollstonecraft Detective Agency” series written by Jordan Stratford and illustrated by Kelly Murphy. Full of literary in-jokes and engagingly arch, this debut novel requires that we suspend historical fastidiousness. We must agree to accept that the real-life daughter of Lord Byron, the future Ada Lovelace (the world’s first computer programmer), and the future Mary Shelley (author of “Frankenstein”), are about the same age...
