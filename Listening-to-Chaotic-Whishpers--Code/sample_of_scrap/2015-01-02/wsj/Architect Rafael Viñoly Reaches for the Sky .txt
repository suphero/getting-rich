Architect Rafael Viñoly Reaches for the Sky Rafael Viñoly walks into his office’s conference room with four pairs of glasses hanging around his neck. One is for reading music to play on one of the nine pianos he owns. Others are for distance, reading and using the computer. They’re “an affectation,” the 70-year-old architect admits with a chuckle. “It’s one of those things you do so people can remember you.” But they’re practical, too. “When people my age see me, they say, ‘How could I not have thought of that?’ ”
