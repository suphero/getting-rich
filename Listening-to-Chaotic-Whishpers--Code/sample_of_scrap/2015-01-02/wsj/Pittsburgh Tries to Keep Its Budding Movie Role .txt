Pittsburgh Tries to Keep Its Budding Movie Role PITTSBURGH—This former steel city has become a center of movie and television production, but the uncertain future of state tax incentives has advocates concerned that the region could soon lose that starring role.Spending on film and television projects in this city hit $150 million last year, up from about $10 million a decade ago, according to the Pittsburgh Film Office, an economic-development nonprofit.
