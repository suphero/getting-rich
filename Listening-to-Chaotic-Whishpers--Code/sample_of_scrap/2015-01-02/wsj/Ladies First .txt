Ladies First  Wild women played an important part in the cultural imagination of ancient Greek, and later Roman, societies. Stories about Amazons—fierce, one-breasted women on horseback—tantalized the male elite of classical Athens with the idea of a culture in which gender roles were the opposite of their own. The reputation of women in wealthy Athenian households depended on their spending most of their time shut up inside, emerging only for religious festivals and funerals. By contrast, Amazons roamed free outside. They could be rulers or queens of their people and excelled in riding, running, hunting and the arts of war....
