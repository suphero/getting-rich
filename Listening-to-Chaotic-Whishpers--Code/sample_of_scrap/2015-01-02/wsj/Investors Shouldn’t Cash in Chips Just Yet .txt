Investors Shouldn’t Cash in Chips Just Yet Makers of smartphones and mobile apps command revved-up valuations these days. But investors are showing a strong preference for the stuff that is under the hood.Semiconductor stocks have been on a tear. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index rose by 28% in 2014, outperforming every other technology group. The sector also made big strides in 2013, bringing the index’s two-year gain to 79% versus 44% for the S&P 500.
