The Victorian Inventions That Time Forgot Need to jump from a burning building? A clever 19th-century inventor has just the thing for you: a basket that releases parachutes for an easy descent. The device, along with hundreds of others registered in the British National Archives, lives on in the new book “Inventions That Didn’t Change the World” (Thames & Hudson, $30) by Julie Halls. Many of the contraptions may seem silly today, such as a currant-cleaning machine or a “portable smelting apparatus” that would allow you to meld metal on the run.
