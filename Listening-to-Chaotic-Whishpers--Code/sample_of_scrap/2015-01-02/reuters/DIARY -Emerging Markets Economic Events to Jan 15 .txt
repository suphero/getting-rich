PUNE, India - Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi is scheduled to hold discussions with the bankers to draw an action plan for reforms in public sector banks (PSBs). The meeting will see discussions on the issues like capital requirement, risk profiling, debt recovery and restructuring of the PSBs to improve their efficiency. The meeting is also likely to focus on what has gone wrong and what should be done both by the banks as well as by the Government to improve and consolidate the position of PSBs. The meeting is likely to be attended by Union Finance Minister Arun Jaitley, Minister of State for Finance Jayant Sinha, RBI Governor Raghuram Rajan and Finance Secretary Rajiv Mehrishi along with several other officials.
