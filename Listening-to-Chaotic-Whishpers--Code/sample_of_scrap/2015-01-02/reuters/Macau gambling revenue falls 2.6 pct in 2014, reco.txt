Macau gambling revenue falls 2.6 pct in 2014, record 30.4 pct in December HONG KONG, Jan 2 (Reuters) - Gambling revenue in the Chinese territory of Macau, the world’s biggest gambling hub, fell 2.6 percent to 351.5 billion patacas ($44.1 billion) in 2014, for the first annual decline since casinos were liberalized there in 2001. Revenue also fell a record 30.4 percent in December from a year earlier to 23.3 billion patacas, marking the seventh consecutive monthly decline, showed data released on Friday by Macau’s Gaming Inspection and Coordination Bureau. The southern territory, a special administrative region like neighbouring Hong Kong, is the only place in China where casino gambling is legal.
