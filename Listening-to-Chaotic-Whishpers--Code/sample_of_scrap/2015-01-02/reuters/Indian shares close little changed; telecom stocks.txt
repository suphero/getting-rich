Indian shares close little changed; telecom stocks surge MUMBAI, Jan 1 (Reuters) - Indian shares ended little changed on the first trading day of 2015 as gains in telecom stocks such as Bharti Airtel on news of spectrum pricing were offset by profit-taking in some blue-chips. The benchmark BSE index gained 0.03 percent to 27,507.54, while the broader NSE index ended 0.02 percent higher at 8,284. Telecom stocks rose after the industry regulator on Wednesday put the 3G spectrum base rate at a 19 percent discount to 2010 auction prices. However, some blue-chips such as NTPC fell 1.1 percent on profit taking. The stock gained 3.4 percent in the three sessions till Wednesday.
