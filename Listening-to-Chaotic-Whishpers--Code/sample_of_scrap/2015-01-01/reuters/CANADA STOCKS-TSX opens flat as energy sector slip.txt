CANADA STOCKS-TSX opens flat as energy sector slips, banks rise TORONTO, Dec 31 (Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index was little changed on Wednesday as a drop in the price of oil that pulled down energy stocks was largely offset by small gains in financial shares. The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX composite index  was down 5.75 points, or 0.04 percent, at 14,634.29 shortly after the open.
