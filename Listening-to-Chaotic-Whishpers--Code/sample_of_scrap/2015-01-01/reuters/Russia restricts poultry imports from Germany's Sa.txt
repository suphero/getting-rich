Russia restricts poultry imports from Germany's Saxony-Anhalt state MOSCOW, Dec 31 (Reuters) - Russia has introduced temporary restrictions on poultry imports from Germany’s Saxony-Anhalt state due to an outbreak of bird flu strain H5N8, its safety watchdog Rosselkhoznadzor said on Wednesday. Earlier this year, Russia banned most Western food imports, worth around $9 billion a year in total, in response to U.S. and European Union sanctions over Moscow’s role in the Ukraine conflict.
