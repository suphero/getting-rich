A Playwright’s Iron Will  Horton Foote’s “The Traveling Lady” opened on Broadway in October 1954 and closed three weeks later. Forty-three years later, “The Young Man from Atlanta” made it to Broadway after having had a solid off-Broadway run and won the Pulitzer Prize. In between . . . nothing. Not only were none of Foote’s three dozen other plays produced on Broadway during that time, but none of them received major productions anywhere in America between 1954 and 1984.
