Besides Lifestyle and Inherited Genes, Cancer Risk Also Tied to Bad Luck Poor lifestyle choices and inherited genes are known to raise a person’s risk of developing cancer. But new research concludes that the majority of our risk across cancer types is due to another factor: bad luck when our cells divide.
